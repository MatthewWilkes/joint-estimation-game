Debriefing

Thank you for your participation in our study! Your anonymous data makes an important contribution to our understanding of human learning and behavior.

During this study, we are looking to understand how people learn to cooperate with one another. Previous research has shown that people working together tend to calibrate their behavior to match their partner's. Our experiment hopes to answer whether this calibration can occur even for perceptual or low-level sensory behaviors. In other words, we want to see whether you and your partner tended to become more similar and more accurate in your estimations of line lengths during the course of the experiment.

If you have any questions about this research, you may contact the researcher, Dr. Alexandra Paxton at paxton.alexandra@berkeley.edu.
